Founding Story â€“ Babatunde Vaughan

Looking back on all that I have achieved, I attribute my success to the strong foundation of faith and family values that I was raised on. I have met many others who share these same convictions and beliefs, and I believe that it has been a key factor in our achievements.

Growing up, my parents exemplified leadership, service, excellence, and integrity. My late father was a man who would drop us off at home from church and then return to pick up strangers from nearby junctions to their homes in Felele, Ibadan, Oyo, Nigeria, especially on rainy days and evenings. He served as the chairman of the Landlord and Tenants Association, and he was committed to serving others. He defended the voiceless and gave roofs to those who did not have them. My mother welcomed every guest into our home with open arms and made sure everyone felt valued. Our landline was available to anyone who needed to use it, and there was no discrimination.

My experience was not unique. Many of my friends grew up in households where parents collectively instilled strong morals and values. Lying, being rude, and stealing were not tolerated, and helping others was always encouraged. Apprentices were taught with excellence as the premium, and their ceremonial "freedom" would be celebrated alongside convocation. However, this narrative has changed over time.

In 2012, while praying in my office, I realized the need to restore declining values and bridge the gap between skills and values. Holistic education was the way forward, not just academic learning that failed to prepare students for life and work. Education without values enabled people to cheat and steal and justify their actions as legal.

Since our kick-off in 2012, we have worked tirelessly towards this collective vision. Today, I lead a team of volunteers and staff who are passionate about this cause, and we are continually inspired by the positive impact of our work. Seeing the lives of young people, we have empowered; families and orphanages impacted give us hope and joy, and we are grateful for the opportunity to lead this conversation on values in Nigeria.
